## Section 2 — "Search is changing"

**Eyebrow:** THE PROBLEM

**Headline (H2):**
Search isn't what it used to be.

**Intro paragraph:**
Five years ago, people found a local business by typing its name into Google and clicking the first blue link. That's not how it works anymore. Today, the same question gets asked three different ways — and an old website usually lands in none of them.

**Three cards (left to right, icons optional):**

### Card 1 — Google answers the question itself.
When someone searches "best taco shop near Keller," Google often answers at the top of the page — pulling text directly from websites whose content is structured to be read by its AI. Sites that aren't structured this way get skipped. Your future customer gets the answer without ever seeing your name.

### Card 2 — ChatGPT and Claude are the new search bar.
A growing share of local searches now happen inside AI assistants — "find me a reliable lawn service in Southlake" — and these tools only quote websites they can read cleanly. If your site buries its content in heavy JavaScript or lacks structured data, it's invisible. Your competitors who built for AI get the recommendation.

### Card 3 — Siri and Alexa want one answer, not ten.
When someone asks their phone "who does tile work near me," the assistant doesn't read off ten blue links — it picks one. The one that gets picked has clean schema data, proper Google Business Profile integration, and FAQ content structured for voice. Most local sites have none of those.

**Closing line + CTA (centered below the three cards):**
Not sure where your website stands? Our free audit shows you exactly what's missing — in 60 seconds, no sign-up.

## Section 5 — "What's inside every build"

**Eyebrow:** THE TECHNICAL FOUNDATION

**Headline (H2):**
Most designers stop at the pretty. We don't.

**Intro:**
Every Sequel Web Studio site — whether new or modernized — is built on a six-layer technical foundation. This is what separates a site that looks good from a site that actually gets found, converts visitors into customers, and keeps working five years from now.

### Layer 1 — Discoverability
*Your business shows up wherever people are searching.*
- **Schema.org structured data** (LocalBusiness, FAQPage, Review, plus industry-specific types like Restaurant, Service) — tells Google and AI tools exactly what your business is and what it offers
- **llms.txt file** — the emerging standard for telling AI assistants (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity) what they're allowed to quote from your site
- **Robots.txt configured for AI crawlers** — explicit opt-in for GPTBot, ClaudeBot, Google-Extended, PerplexityBot
- **Google Business Profile integration** with consistent Name/Address/Phone across the web
- **Open Graph and Twitter card tags** so your site looks clean when shared in texts and on social
- **FAQ content structured for voice search** — so Siri and Alexa can quote you

### Layer 2 — Conversion
*Visitors become customers, not just pageviews.*
- **One clear call-to-action on every page** — no clutter, no dead ends
- **Tappable phone numbers on mobile** — one touch to call
- **Embedded booking widget** tuned to your business type (OpenTable, Calendly, Square Appointments — whatever fits)
- **Short contact form** that emails you directly
- **Trust signals designed in** — Google review count, years in business, certifications, badges
- **Embedded Google Map** with hours, address, and parking notes

### Layer 3 — Foundation
*The technical basics, done right.*
- **Sub-1-second page loads on mobile** — Google PageSpeed Insights score 95+ out of the box
- **Mobile-first responsive design** — because most local searches happen on a phone
- **Semantic HTML** — machine-readable structure
- **XML sitemap** submitted to Google
- **Image optimization** — WebP format, lazy loading, responsive sizing
- **Clean URL structure** — readable to humans and search engines
- **HTTPS/SSL encryption** — automatic, always on

### Layer 4 — Measurement
*You can see it working.*
- **Google Analytics 4 with conversion events** — we track actual actions (form submitted, phone called), not just pageviews
- **Google Search Console set up and connected** — so you can see which searches bring you visitors
- **Monthly one-page report** delivered to your inbox, in plain English
- **Uptime monitoring** — if the site goes down, you get an email

### Layer 5 — Legal & Compliance
*Built to the standard regulators now expect.*
- **Privacy policy** — required because Google Analytics collects visitor data
- **Cookie consent banner** — GDPR and CCPA ready
- **HTTPS encryption** — required by modern browsers; often missing on older sites

### Layer 6 — Longevity
*A site that still works five years from now.*
- **Branded 404 page** so lost visitors don't bounce
- **Editable sections** — you can update hours, menu items, and photos yourself without calling us
- **Clean handoff documentation** — any developer can maintain your site if we ever part ways

**Closing line (callout, right-aligned, purple accent):**
When a graphic designer builds you a website, you get the first layer. When we build you a website, you get all six.
